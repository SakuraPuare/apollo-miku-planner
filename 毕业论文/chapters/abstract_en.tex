\noindent\hspace{2em}{\bfseries Abstract: }In dense multi-obstacle scenarios, existing autonomous driving path planning algorithms suffer from path blockage, conservative decisions, and low traffic efficiency. Based on Baidu's Apollo open-source autonomous driving platform, this thesis conducts a source-code-level analysis of its Planning module and identifies three planning deficiencies in PathBoundsDecider: a uniform safety buffer that unnecessarily compresses the passage band, independent per-obstacle nudge decisions that produce contradictory avoidance directions, and complete exclusion of dynamic obstacles from path decisions leading to path-speed coordination failure.

To address these problems, this thesis proposes the Multi-obstacle Interaction-density Kinematic-prediction Unified Planner (MIKU) algorithm, which formulates multi-obstacle nudge direction selection as a combinatorial optimization problem. For safety margins, a multi-factor threat assessment model fusing time-to-collision, lateral overlap, relative velocity, obstacle type, and interaction density replaces Apollo's uniform buffer with differentiated margins $\delta_i$. For decision granularity, a sweep-line algorithm clusters longitudinally interacting obstacles into connected components on which globally optimal nudge decisions are made; the contiguity of the optimal partition in lateral order (lemma) and the optimality of the maximum-gap strategy (theorem) are proved, reducing the search space from $O(2^k)$ to $O(k \log k)$. For spatiotemporal coordination, arrival-time functions and predicted trajectories bring dynamic obstacles into path boundary decisions; the resulting time-varying passage band is projected into a spatiotemporal passable corridor whose boundary constraints are fused with SpeedBoundsDecider's Station-Time (ST) boundaries at the speed Quadratic Programming (QP) level, achieving path-speed coordinated planning.

Simulation results demonstrate that compared with Apollo's native PiecewiseJerkPathOptimizer, MIKU successfully traverses all four multi-obstacle test scenarios whereas Baseline fails in every case, increases average traffic speed from \MSummaryBaselineAvgV\,m/s to \MSummaryMikuAvgV\,m/s (an improvement of \MSummarySummaryVImprovement\%), and reduces total QP solving time from \MSummaryBaselineQpTotal\,ms to \MSummaryMikuQpTotal\,ms, with $O(n \log n)$ computational complexity satisfying real-time requirements.
